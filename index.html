About Me

I am a Ph.D. student with a passion for teaching and making complex scientific concepts accessible to all. I believe that knowledge thrives when shared, and I enjoy simplifying intricate ideas to enhance learning.

My research focuses on cosmic ray muons and atmospheric effects, particularly exploring high-energy cosmic ray interactions. Beyond research, I am keenly interested in developing advanced data acquisition systems using Raspberry Pi and Arduino for cosmic ray detection. Additionally, I have hands-on experience in statistical data analysis using Python and ROOT CERN.

With a strong inclination toward both academia and industry, I aspire to contribute to cutting-edge research and development in experimental physics while inspiring the next generation of scientists.
